  • The Lean Startup by Eric Ries

  • The Road to Character by David Brooks
    • Date Read: 2015.12 - 2016.02
    • Listened to the audiobook
    • It was a nice book with a lot of examples of people that had characters. I like overall idea of the book and what it promotes, but I was not convinced by some of the examples it had. It made me think that US society is divided in a lot of parts, and the part that is non-South thinking (I don't know how else to discribe it), and has achieved something has a lot of values that that my parents taught me. So even though American mentality is far away from Armenian one, there is a set of core values of being human that is shared among both, but you have to dig for those and you only find those values in thinkers of the time.

  • Solaris by Stanislaw Lem
    • Date Read: 2015.01
    • Very entertaining, my fist Lem novel. Written from first person. After Of Mice and Men I've started noticing the way story is presented and how could it be converted to a movie so I should watch Solaris (2002) and see how they did.
    • I really didn’t like Solaris (2002) movie, it tried to take some ideas out of the book (I didn’t get all of those ideas, but most of them) and force feeds to the audience. It solves all the narration by skipping it and creating a slightly different back story which doesn’t make much sense.
    • (2015.12) I watched Andrei Tarkovsky's Solaris (1972) and I liked it a lot. I think the movies made me look at the book in a slightly different way. The quote that I remembered from the movies was that we were looking for a contact with ET civilization but when we find it we have no idea what to do with it. Another good one was that human race doesn't want to conquer the universe, it wants to expand Earth to the boundaries of the universe. Great movie! I think I should read the book again in a year or so.
